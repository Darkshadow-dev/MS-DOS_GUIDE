The History of MS-DOS

MS-DOS (Microsoft Disk Operating System) was a groundbreaking operating system developed for IBM-compatible personal computers. Here’s a detailed look at its history:

Origins and Development

  • When: First released in August 1981.
  • Who: Developed by Microsoft, led by Bill Gates and Paul Allen.
  • How: Based on QDOS (Quick and Dirty Operating System), which Microsoft purchased and modified.
  • Why: Built for IBM’s first personal computer.

Timeline of Commands and Features

  • 1981: Basic commands like dir, copy, del.
  • 1983: MS-DOS 2.0 added directories, mkdir, cd.
  • 1986: MS-DOS 3.2 added networking and net.
  • 1991: MS-DOS 5.0 added the edit text editor.
  • 1993: MS-DOS 6.22 added scandisk and disk compression tools.

Legacy and Importance

MS-DOS became the foundation for early versions of Microsoft Windows and remained widely used throughout the 1980s and 1990s. Its simplicity and flexibility helped shape modern computing.
